3. "How should hospitals handle medication errors?"
answer:
Based on the context provided, hospitals should handle medication errors as follows:

From MM.6:
1. Develop policies and procedures to minimize drug errors (SR.1)
2. Establish procedures for reporting:
- Transfusion reactions
- Adverse drug reactions
- Errors in prescribing, preparing, and administering drugs (SR.2)

3. Immediately report medication-related errors to the attending physician, if appropriate (SR.3)

4. Track and analyze all medication errors to determine their causes (SR.4)

From QM.8, additional guidance includes:
5. Use a systemic approach to:
- Determine the cause of the error
- Implement appropriate preventive actions

6. Develop improvement strategies such as:
- Changing policies and procedures
- Repairing/replacing equipment
- Providing staff education and training

7. Adopt a non-punitive reporting system focused on the system, not individual healthcare professionals

8. Measure the effectiveness of the reporting system using established benchmarks or published studies

9. Ensure staff are aware of the reporting process and can identify different types of medication errors

The goal is to learn from errors, prevent future occurrences, and continuously improve patient safety.
